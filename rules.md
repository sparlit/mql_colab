## Method 1.1: Technical Analysis

**Magic Number:** `0x4D01-TA-0001`

**Definition:** Analysis of price action through historical price charts and technical indicators to predict future price movements.

**Core Principles:**

- Price discounts all known information
- Price movements are not entirely random
- History repeats itself in patterns

**Key Tools:** Charts, indicators, oscillators, pattern recognition

**Timeframe Applicability:** All timeframes (M1 to MN)

**Skill Level Required:** Intermediate

**Data Requirements:** OHLCV (Open, High, Low, Close, Volume) data

**Limitations:** Does not account for sudden fundamental shocks; lagging indicators produce delayed signals

---

## Method 1.2: Fundamental Analysis

**Magic Number:** `0x4D02-FA-0002`

**Definition:** Evaluation of economic, social, and political forces that affect currency supply and demand.

**Core Principles:**

- Currency value reflects economic health
- Interest rate differentials drive capital flows
- Macroeconomic indicators predict long-term trends

**Key Data Points:**

- Central bank interest rates (Fed, ECB, BOJ, BOE, SNB, RBA, RBNZ, BOC)
- GDP growth rates
- Inflation metrics (CPI, PPI, Core PCE)
- Employment data (NFP, Unemployment Rate, Jobless Claims)
- Trade balance figures
- Political stability indices

**Timeframe Applicability:** H4 to MN (long-term)

**Skill Level Required:** Advanced

**Data Requirements:** Economic calendar, central bank statements, government statistics

**Limitations:** Timing of entry/exit is imprecise; requires deep economic knowledge

---

## Method 1.3: Price Action Trading

**Magic Number:** `0x4D03-PA-0003`

**Definition:** Trading based on raw price movement without lagging indicators, relying solely on candlestick patterns and chart structures.

**Core Principles:**

- Price is the only truth
- Indicators are derivatives of price (redundant)
- Market structure reveals intent

**Key Components:**

- Candlestick patterns (engulfing, pin bar, doji, hammer, shooting star)
- Support and resistance levels
- Market structure (higher highs, lower lows)
- Supply and demand zones
- Liquidity pools

**Timeframe Applicability:** M15 to D1 (optimal)

**Skill Level Required:** Intermediate to Advanced

**Data Requirements:** OHLC data only

**Limitations:** Subjective interpretation; requires significant screen time experience

---

## Method 1.4: Algorithmic Trading

**Magic Number:** `0x4D04-AT-0004`

**Definition:** Execution of trading rules via computer programs, removing human emotion and enabling high-frequency analysis.

**Core Principles:**

- Rules-based execution eliminates emotional bias
- Backtesting validates strategies before deployment
- Speed advantages over manual trading

**Key Components:**

- Strategy definition (entry/exit rules)
- Backtesting engine
- Risk management module
- Execution engine (API connectivity)
- Monitoring and alerting system

**Programming Languages:** Python, MQL4, MQL5, C++, R, Java

**Key Libraries:** pandas, numpy, ta-lib, backtrader, zipline, vectorbt

**Timeframe Applicability:** All timeframes (M1 to MN)

**Skill Level Required:** Advanced (programming + trading knowledge)

**Data Requirements:** Tick data or OHLCV, low-latency feed for HFT

**Limitations:** Overfitting in backtests; infrastructure costs; system failure risk

## Method 1.5: Scalping

**Magic Number:** `0x4D05-SC-0005`

**Definition:** High-frequency trading method taking small profits from numerous trades within very short timeframes.

## Strategy 2.13: Pivot Point Bounce

**Magic Number:** `0x4S13-PPB-0013`

**Method Base:** Technical Analysis (`0x4D01-TA-0001`)

**Timeframe:** M15 (entry), H1 (confirmation)

**Indicators:** Daily Pivot Points (PP, R1, R2, R3, S1, S2, S3), RSI(14)

**Pivot Point Calculation:**

```
PP = (High + Low + Close) / 3
R1 = (2 × PP) - Low
S1 = (2 × PP) - High
R2 = PP + (High - Low)
S2 = PP - (High - Low)
R3 = High + 2 × (PP - Low)
S3 = Low - 2 × (High - PP)
```

**Entry Rules:**

- **Long:** Price approaches S1 or S2 from above + RSI oversold + bullish reversal candlestick
- **Short:** Price approaches R1 or R2 from below + RSI overbought + bearish reversal candlestick

**Exit Rules:**

- Next pivot level reached
- PP level (if trading from S1/R1)

**Stop Loss:** Beyond the pivot level being traded + 10 pips

**Take Profit:** Next pivot level (S1→PP, PP→R1, R1→R2, etc.)

**Risk Management:** 0.75% risk per trade

**Win Rate (Backtest):** 46-53%

**Reward-to-Risk:** 1.5:1 to 2:1

**Strengths:** Objective levels; respected by institutional traders; daily reset

**Weaknesses:** Less effective on trend days; pivot calculation varies by method

## 4.3 Forex Pricing Concepts

### Pip

- Smallest standard price movement
- Most pairs: 0.0001 (4th decimal)
- JPY pairs: 0.01 (2nd decimal)
- Some brokers offer pipettes (5th decimal): 0.00001

### Lot Sizes

| Lot Type | Units | Pip Value (USD pairs) | Margin (1:100) |
|----------|-------|----------------------|----------------|
| Standard | 100,000 | $10.00 | $1,000 |
| Mini | 10,000 | $1.00 | $100 |
| Micro | 1,000 | $0.10 | $10 |
| Nano | 100 | $0.01 | $1 |

### Bid/Ask Spread

- **Bid:** Price you can sell at
- **Ask:** Price you can buy at
- **Spread:** Difference between bid and ask
- Spread = Transaction cost
- Variable spreads widen during news/events
- Fixed spreads have premium built-in

### Margin and Leverage

- **Margin:** Deposit required to open position
- **Leverage:** Multiplier of buying power
- **Formula:** `Margin = Notional Value / Leverage`
- **Example:** 1 standard lot EUR/USD at 1:100 leverage = $1,000 margin
- **Margin Call:** Triggered when equity falls below margin requirement
- **Stop Out:** Broker closes positions when equity falls below stop-out level (typically 50% or 20% of margin)

### Swap/Rollover

- Interest paid/received for holding positions overnight
- Calculated from interest rate differential between currencies
- Triple swap on Wednesday (accounts for weekend)
- Can be positive (earn) or negative (pay)
- Significant for carry trade strategy and swing/position trading

## 4.5 Risk Management Principles

### Position Sizing Formula

```
Position Size = (Account Balance × Risk %) / (Stop Loss in Pips × Pip Value)
```

### Risk Rules

1. Maximum 1-2% risk per trade
2. Maximum 5% total portfolio risk at any time
3. Maximum 3-5 correlated positions
4. Minimum 1:2 reward-to-risk ratio
5. Always use stop loss orders
6. Never move stop loss away from entry
7. Reduce position size after consecutive losses

### Drawdown Management

| Drawdown Level | Action Required |
|----------------|-----------------|
| 0-10% | Normal trading |
| 10-20% | Reduce position size by 50% |
| 20-30% | Stop trading, review strategy |
| 30%+ | Close all positions, complete system review |

### Correlation Risk

- **Highly correlated pairs** (EUR/USD and GBP/USD: ~0.85)
  - Taking same direction = doubled risk
- **Negatively correlated pairs** (EUR/USD and USD/CHF: ~-0.95)
  - Taking same direction = hedged (no profit potential)
- Always check correlation before taking multiple positions

## 4.8 Trading Psychology

### Common Psychological Biases

| Bias | Description | Impact |
|------|-------------|--------|
| Loss Aversion | Losses hurt 2x more than equivalent gains feel good | Holding losers too long |
| Confirmation Bias | Seeking information that confirms existing beliefs | Ignoring contrary evidence |
| Recency Bias | Overweighting recent events | Chasing trends, fear after losses |
| Anchoring | Fixating on specific price levels | Unrealistic profit targets |
| Overconfidence | Overestimating ability after wins | Excessive risk taking |
| Revenge Trading | Increasing size to recover losses | Catastrophic drawdowns |
| FOMO (Fear of Missing Out) | Entering late due to fear of missing move | Poor entries, chasing |
| Disposition Effect | Selling winners too early, holding losers | Reduced profits, increased losses |

### Psychological Management Techniques

1. **Trading Journal:** Document every trade with emotions, reasoning, outcome
2. **Pre-Trade Checklist:** Standardized entry criteria to reduce impulsive trades
3. **Meditation/Mindfulness:** Daily practice to improve emotional regulation
4. **Physical Exercise:** Reduces stress, improves cognitive function
5. **Adequate Sleep:** 7-9 hours essential for decision-making
6. **Social Support:** Trading community or mentor for accountability
7. **Take Breaks:** Step away after consecutive losses or high-stress trades
8. **Process Over Outcome:** Judge yourself on following rules, not on individual trade results
9. **Position Size Comfort:** Never trade sizes that cause anxiety
10. **Accept Uncertainty:** The market is probabilistic, not certain

### Trading Plan Template Elements

1. Trading goals (realistic, measurable)
2. Trading schedule (sessions, days)
3. Markets traded (specific pairs with justification)
4. Strategy definitions (entry, exit, risk rules)
5. Risk management rules (position sizing, max drawdown)
6. Pre-market routine
7. During-market rules
8. Post-market review process
9. Performance metrics tracked
10. Continuous improvement process

| Win Rate | Wins / Total Trades | 40-60% (strategy dependent) |
| Profit Factor | Gross Profit / Gross Loss | > 1.5 |
| Expectancy | (Win% × Avg Win) - (Loss% × Avg Loss) | > 0 |
| Sharpe Ratio | (Return - Risk Free Rate) / Std Dev of Returns | > 1.0 |
| Sortino Ratio | (Return - Risk Free Rate) / Downside Deviation | > 1.5 |
| Max Drawdown | (Peak - Trough) / Peak | < 20% |
| Average Risk-Reward | Avg Win / Avg Loss | > 1.5 |
| Calmar Ratio | Annual Return / Max Drawdown | > 1.0 |
| Recovery Factor | Net Profit / Max Drawdown | > 3.0 |
| Trade Frequency | Trades per period | Strategy dependent |

### Equity Curve Analysis

- **Smooth upward curve** = consistent strategy
- **Deep drawdowns** = risk management issues
- **Flat periods** = strategy not suited for current market
- **Spike up then spike down** = overfitting or lucky streak

### Monte Carlo Simulation

- Tests strategy robustness by randomizing trade sequence
- Generates distribution of possible outcomes
- Identifies worst-case scenarios
- 1,000+ iterations recommended
- Focus on 5th percentile outcome for risk planning

## 4.10 Common Forex Scams and Red Flags

### Scam Types

1. **Guaranteed Returns:** No one can guarantee forex profits
2. **Signal Services (Fake):** Fabricated track records
3. **Managed Accounts (Fraudulent):** Manager disappears with funds
4. **Forex Robots (Scam):** Backtested curves that don't work live
5. **Unregulated Brokers:** Cannot withdraw funds
6. **Binary Options:** Often manipulated, many jurisdictions banned
7. **Pump and Dump:** On exotic/illiquid pairs
8. **Phony Education:** Overpriced courses with recycled content

### Red Flags

- Promises of consistent 50%+ monthly returns
- Pressure to deposit immediately
- No verifiable track record (Myfxbook, etc.)
- Unregulated or offshore-only broker
- No clear risk disclosure
- Complex fee structures
- Difficulty withdrawing funds
- Social media "lifestyle" marketing

### Verification Resources

- **Myfxbook.com** - Verified track records
- **Forex Peace Army** - Broker reviews
- **Regulatory websites** - FCA register, NFA Background Affiliation Status
- **LinkedIn** - Verification of credentials
